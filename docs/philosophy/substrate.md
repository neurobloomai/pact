## How to Build Relational Intelligence (RQ) 

✅ Coherence emerges over TIME → sequential
✅ Alignment happens across MODALITIES (text, tone, body language) → multi-modal
✅ Meaning depends on CONTEXT (relationship history, cultural norms) → contextual

But here's what's missing:
You're describing the technical requirements (what the architecture needs to do).
You're not describing the substrate requirements (what makes it actually achieve coherence/alignment).
The Completion:

"For modeling coherence and alignment, we need architectures that can handle sequential, multi-modal, and contextual data while maintaining relational memory and co-regulatory feedback loops that track emotional state transitions across time."

Why this matters:
Current architectures (Transformers, LSTMs, multi-modal models) CAN handle sequential, multi-modal, contextual data.
But they don't inherently track:

Emotional coherence (is the person moving toward or away from alignment?)
Relational patterns (how does this person typically regulate emotion?)
Co-regulation dynamics (how does my response affect their state?)

That's what PACT adds. The architecture might be standard, but the what you track within it is novel.
The Better Framing:
"Standard architectures handle sequential, multi-modal, contextual data. But for true coherence and alignment, we need infrastructure that tracks emotional state transitions, relational patterns, and co-regulatory dynamics - that's what PACT provides."
In other words:
The architecture is the cage (the rules).
PACT is the ingenuity that makes it actually serve relational intelligence.
